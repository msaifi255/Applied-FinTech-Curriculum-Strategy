Applied FinTech Curriculum Strategy

Addressing the Jordanian Financial Sector's Skills Requirements

1. Industry Challenge: Digital Skills Gap

Jordan's financial sector is rapidly digitizing, creating a gap between traditional academic outputs and industry needs. Banks increasingly require technical and analytical skills for entry-level roles, moving beyond conventional operational positions.

Key In-Demand Competencies:

  • Analytical Thinking & Digital Literacy
  • Data Analysis & AI/ML Familiarity (>50% of entry roles)
  • Effective Communication

Source: Association of Banks in Jordan (ABJ) Study [See Ref. 1]

2. Conceptual Approach: Applied Science

The curriculum prioritizes an **Applied Science** model, focusing on equipping graduates with the ability to utilize existing technologies and data to solve specific, real-world financial problems, rather than focusing purely on theoretical development.

Focus Applied Science (Curriculum Goal)
Goal Solve business problems; deliver solutions.
Skill Tool proficiency; Domain integration.
Output Actionable models, insights, products.

3. Proposed Solution: The 30 C.H. FinTech Minor

A focused 10-course minor designed for Finance/Business majors within a standard 132 C.H. program. Aims to develop a **FinTech Subject Matter Expert (SME)** capable of bridging business needs and technical possibilities.

Finance Major Core + 30 C.H. Applied Tech Minor = Market-Ready FinTech Innovator

4. Curriculum Structure (30 Credit Hours)

The minor progresses through three phases: foundational technical literacy, core application skills, and innovation leadership.

Phase 1: Foundations (6 C.H.)

Python Scripting, SQL & Data Management.

Phase 2: Core Applications (15 C.H.)

Digital Payments/APIs, Blockchain Concepts, ML for Risk/Fraud, Advanced Financial Modeling, Cybersecurity/RegTech.

Phase 3: Innovation (9 C.H.)

BI & Data Storytelling, AI Applications & Ethics, Capstone Project.

Credit Hour Distribution by Phase

5. Market Alignment: Targeted Job Roles in Jordan

The curriculum is directly mapped to specific high-demand roles within Jordan's evolving financial sector.

Target Job Role Key Minor Skills/Courses Relevance
Digital Transformation Officer Payments/APIs, RegTech, AI Apps Drives tech integration strategy [Ref. 5].
Financial Data / BI Analyst SQL, Python, BI/Storytelling Core data analysis & reporting needs [Ref. 1, 2].
Tech Risk / Compliance Analyst ML for Risk, Cybersecurity/RegTech Manages risk using tech; ensures compliance [Ref. 3].
FinTech Product Owner Capstone, Payments/APIs, AI Apps Defines & guides FinTech product development [Ref. 4].

6. Validation: Applied Innovation Case Study

A marketing professional, with minimal Python training, successfully automated internal team processes. This initiative filled an operational need unmet by the core development team, leading to recognition and a new role facilitating collaboration between business and technical units.

"This demonstrates the significant value created when business professionals can directly apply basic technology skills to solve domain-specific problems, embodying the FinTech SME."

The proposed minor cultivates this capability, preparing graduates to identify opportunities and implement practical, tech-enabled solutions.
