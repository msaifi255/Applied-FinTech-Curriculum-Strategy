Why This Matters

Jordan's financial ecosystem is digitizing faster than talent pipelines.

Insights from the Association of Banks in Jordan, MoDEE/World Bank, and CBJ vision documents show urgent demand for hybrid business-technology talent. This experience blueprint translates those signals into deliberate curriculum moves.

Signals from Industry

Digital transformation mandates

Banks require teams that translate strategic priorities into data-driven initiatives without always deferring to core IT teams.

Talent shortage in analytics

More than half of entry roles now demand SQL, Python, and dashboard storytelling capabilities.

RegTech and risk pressures

Compliance functions expect teams to leverage AI, automation, and regulatory sandboxes to keep pace with CBJ directives.

Learner Personas

Finance Major + Digital Curiosity

Comfortable with spreadsheets, keen to translate insights into business cases.

Business Analyst in Training

Seeks structured exposure to APIs, automation, and product experimentation.

Future Compliance Innovator

Interested in RegTech, policy sandboxes, and resilience testing.

The program is built to transform these personas into FinTech SMEs who drive outcomes across product, risk, and analytics teams.

Implementation Roadmap

From vision to launch in four orchestrated cycles.

The roadmap aligns academic governance, talent readiness, and market partnerships to ensure sustainable delivery.

Cycle 1 · Discovery & Co-Design

Stakeholder interviews, curriculum benchmarking (ABJ, MoDEE/WB, CBJ), capability framework draft.

Cycle 2 · Pilot & Iteration

Run two signature sprints with partner banks, capture learner reflections, refine assessments.

Cycle 3 · Launch & Scale

Formalize the 30 C.H. minor, onboard faculty, secure sandbox access, embed employer mentors.

Cycle 4 · Continuous Improvement

Annual design summit with data-driven revisions, new briefs, and credential refresh.
